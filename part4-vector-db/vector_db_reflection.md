## Vector DB Use Case 
 
A traditional keyword-based search would not be sufficient for querying large legal contracts. Keyword search relies on exact word matching, which means it may fail to retrieve relevant information if different wording or synonyms are used. For example, a lawyer searching for “termination clauses” might miss sections written as “agreement cancellation terms” or “contract ending conditions,” even though they refer to the same concept. This limitation makes keyword search inefficient for complex legal documents. 
 
Legal contracts are lengthy and written in formal language, where meaning is often expressed in varied ways. Keyword-based systems cannot understand context or semantic meaning, leading to incomplete or irrelevant results. This becomes a major issue when lawyers need precise and quick access to important clauses. 
 
A vector database solves this problem by enabling semantic search. It converts text into embeddings, which are numerical representations capturing the meaning of the content. When a user asks a question in plain English, the query is also converted into an embedding. The system then compares this with stored embeddings and retrieves the most semantically similar sections, even if the exact keywords are different. 
 
This approach allows for more accurate, context-aware, and efficient search across large documents. Therefore, a vector database plays a crucial role in building intelligent legal search systems where understanding meaning is more important than matching exact words.
